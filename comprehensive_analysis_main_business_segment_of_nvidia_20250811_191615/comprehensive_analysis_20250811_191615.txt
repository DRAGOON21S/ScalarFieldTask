## Key Findings by Source
### Business Fundamentals (10-K Analysis)
The FY2025 10-K filing paints a clear picture of a company re-centered around a single, high-growth engine.

*   **Segment Dominance:** The **Compute & Networking** segment is the company's primary business, generating **$116.2 billion (89.0% of total revenue)**. This is driven by strong demand for Hopper and Blackwell architecture platforms for AI applications.
*   **Legacy Segment:** The **Graphics** segment, which includes GeForce GPUs for gaming and PCs, generated **$14.3 billion (11.0% of total revenue)** and is subject to more traditional consumer market volatility.
*   **Primary Risk Factor:** The 10-K explicitly identifies geopolitical and regulatory actions as the most significant risk. It notes that U.S. export controls on high-performance products (A100, H100, B200) have already caused competitive harm and that future regulations, like the "AI Diffusion" IFR, pose a substantial worldwide threat to sales.

### Material Events (8-K Analysis)
Recent 8-K filings provide concrete evidence of the risks and strategic shifts outlined in the 10-K.

*   **Corporate Direction:** Nvidia is unequivocally an AI company. Its financial health, strategic focus, and market valuation are now almost entirely dependent on the continued growth and dominance of its Compute & Networking segment. The Graphics segment, while historically important, is now a secondary business line.
*   **Overall Health and Trajectory:** The company's financial health is exceptionally strong, characterized by hyper-growth that far outweighs the significant financial charges from regulatory headwinds and past strategic failures. The trajectory is upward, but its path has narrowed, making it less diversified and more vulnerable to shocks within the AI sector.
*   **Key Risks and Opportunities:** The primary opportunity is to maintain its commanding lead in the AI accelerator market. The primary risk, confirmed across all available data, is geopolitical. U.S. export controls are not a future possibility but an active, ongoing, and financially damaging reality that management must navigate to protect its core business.
